# Mom Test Notes: OfficeHoursQ Validation

**Target Segment:** University Computer Science Departments (Students, TAs, and Professors)

## Big 3 Learning Goals
1. How do students currently handle getting help when stuck on assignments, and what is the biggest pain point in that process?
2. How do TAs currently manage the flow of students during office hours, and where does the process break down?
3. How do professors currently track the effectiveness of office hours and student comprehension?

---

## Conversation 1: The Student (Alex, CS Sophomore)

**Background:** Takes Data Structures (CS201), frequently attends office hours for programming assignments.

**Notes:**
- ^ Usually starts assignment 2 days before deadline.
- ⚽ Goal is to get code compiling and pass test cases so they can sleep.
- ☇ "Last week I waited in the basement lab for 3 hours. I put my name on the whiteboard but I had to go to the bathroom and when I came back someone had erased me."
- ⤴ "Now I just buddy up with 3 friends and we all put our names on the board so at least one of us gets called."
- ☐ "The worst part is waiting entirely in the dark. I don't know if the TA is spending 5 minutes or 45 minutes per person. I can't even go get food."
- ☇ "Sometimes a TA finally gets to me, and my issue was just a missing library import that took 10 seconds to fix."
- ☑ "I wish I knew how long the wait was before I walked all the way across campus in the snow."

**Mom Test Analysis:**
- *Good questions asked:* "Talk me through the last time you went to office hours." "How are you dealing with the waiting right now?"
- *Learnings:* The uncertainty of wait time is a bigger pain than the wait time itself. The whiteboard system is actively flawed and encourages gaming the system.

## Conversation 2: The Teaching Assistant (Sarah, Senior CS Major)

**Background:** Head TA for CS201, runs 4 hours of office hours per week.

**Notes:**
- ^ Runs office hours in a 30-person lab room. Usually has 50+ students show up on the day before an assignment is due.
- ⚽ Goal is to help as many students as possible without giving away the direct answers.
- ☇ "It gets incredibly chaotic. Students form a mob around my desk. We tried using a Google Sheet for a queue, but students would just re-sort the rows or type over each other."
- ☐ "I spend the first 5 minutes with every student just trying to understand what file they're looking at and what the error message is."
- ⤴ "I've started telling them to have their error message highlighted on screen before I sit down, but they forget."
- ☇ "The most frustrating thing is realizing I just helped 5 different students with the exact same conceptual misunderstanding about pointers, individually."

**Mom Test Analysis:**
- *Good questions asked:* "What's the hardest part about managing the lab?" "Tell me about the Google Sheet queue."
- *Learnings:* TAs lack context when approaching a student, wasting valuable time. Grouping students by problem type (e.g., "pointers") could massively increase efficiency just by identifying similar issues quickly.

## Conversation 3: The Professor (Dr. Chen)

**Background:** Teaches Intro to Systems, manages a staff of 12 TAs.

**Notes:**
- ^ Doesn't attend TA office hours directly, relies on weekly TA meetings for updates.
- ⚽ Goal is to ensure students are learning the material and TAs are providing consistent, high-quality help.
- ☇ "I honestly have no idea what happens in office hours. I just see the budget for TA hours get eaten up."
- ☐ "After the midterm, I realized half the class completely bombed the memory allocation question. The TAs later told me that everyone was asking about it in office hours all week."
- ⤴ "I ask the TAs to keep a log of common questions in a shared doc, but they are too busy helping students to write things down."
- ＄ "If I had data showing that office hours are consistently understaffed on Wednesdays, it would be much easier to justify requesting more TA budget from the department."

**Mom Test Analysis:**
- *Good questions asked:* "How do you track what students are struggling with?" "Why haven't the shared docs worked?"
- *Learnings:* Professors are flying blind regarding real-time student comprehension. Analytics aren't just a "nice to have" — they are directly tied to pedagogical interventions and resource planning.
